Family Rescue

Breaking the cycle of domestic violence.

Family Rescue is a non-profit organization that provides comprehensive support services and shelter for individuals and families who have experienced domestic violence. They have an emergency shelter as well as a residential program for families to receive social, psychological and educational support, housing and legal guidance, and parenting and life management skill development.

Amy spent July 2015 as an occupational therapy fieldwork student at Family Rescue. While she was there, she created and implemented therapeutic groups for the children’s summer camp program. She organized groups, like yoga and gardening, that introduced the children to new activities while at the same time practicing and developing important skills, such as turn-taking, problem-solving, and fine and gross motor skills. She discovered her passion for working with this population over the summer and is looking forward to hopefully continuing her relationship with Family Rescue during her doctoral year.

Growing Home

Cultivating healthy people and communities through urban organic agriculture.

Growing Home is Chicago’s leading expert in farm-based training for people with employment barriers. Growing Home provides job experience and job readiness training at their farm in Englewood, an underserved community on the South side of Chicago with extremely high rates of unemployment and poverty and limited access to healthy food choices. In addition to job training skills, Growing Home provides the services and support needed to conquer other issues such as criminal records, medical needs, child-care and housing in order to transform and rejuvenate low-income communities.

We first found out about Growing Home when we were looking into different Community Supported Agriculture (CSA) options for the fall after we moved to Chicago. Once we found Growing Home, we immediately fell in love with their mission and signed up! We had a great time cooking with the different veggies from the farm. Last October, we had the chance to visit the farm in Englewood and to meet some of the participants whose lives have been changed by Growing Home. We still follow them and Instagram and hope they'll start up their CSA again soon!

St. Leonard's Ministries

Rebuilding lives. Reshaping futures.

St. Leonard’s Ministries is a non-profit organization that serves women and men who have formerly been incarcerated. Residents typically live at St. Leonard’s for about 6 months. During this time, they enroll in classes and meetings, such as high school courses and vocational training, that provide assistance as they transition into successful, independent housing. St. Leonard’s also provides an environment to develop a healthy social support system and the skills necessary to regain their lives.

During the spring of 2015, Amy spent 6 weeks working with the men at St. Leonard’s residential program. Along with two of her classmates, she taught a life skills course to the residents, discussing topics with them such as goal setting, healthy eating, and time management. Both of us are committed to working within our fields to support people and organizations trying to fix our broken criminal justice system, and St. Leonard's is one of the organizations doing just that.

Fun in the Sun

There are lots of fun things to do in Tampa. So make sure you get out of the hotel and take it all in. Here are some of our suggestions:

Walk on the Riverwalk

A 2.4-mile path along Tampa's Hillsborough River (map) which goes right past the Marriott Waterside. Great for walking--or, rent a bike from Coast Bike Share for just $8/hour.

Rent a water bike

We haven't tried this yet, but it looks like a blast! Pedal down the river in a water bike or a biyak (a pedal kayak) from Tampa Bay Water Bike Co.

Check out Ybor City

Tampa's historic cigar-making district, Ybor City has restaurants & bars, cafes, and classic architecture, plus an outdoor market on Saturdays from 9am - 1pm! You can take a trolley there from the hotel ($2.50 each way).

The Florida Aquarium

Less than a 10-minute walk from the hotel, the aquarium has sharks, rays and—our favorite!—penguins.
